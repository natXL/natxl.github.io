Epic Big Island Vacation Guide

Where to Stay

Kona (West Side): Great for sunny weather, beaches, snorkeling, and proximity to coffee farms.

Hilo (East Side): Ideal for lush rainforests, waterfalls, and exploring Hawaii Volcanoes National Park.

Split Your Stay: Spend a few days on each side to experience the island's diverse offerings.

Must-Do Activities

1. Visit Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

See Kīlauea and Mauna Loa volcanoes. Hike trails like Kīlauea Iki Crater or Thurston Lava Tube. Try stargazing at the summit of Mauna Kea (requires 4WD to reach the top).

2. Snorkeling and Water Adventures

Kealakekua Bay: Snorkel at Captain Cook Monument.

Two Step Beach: Known for vibrant coral and marine life.

Night snorkeling with manta rays near Kona.

3. Beaches

Hapuna Beach: White sand, great for swimming.

Punaluʻu Black Sand Beach: Spot turtles basking in the sun.

Green Sand Beach (Papakōlea): A rare green-sand beach; requires a hike or 4WD.

4. Explore Waterfalls

Rainbow Falls: Near Hilo, easy access.

Akaka Falls: A short hike through lush greenery.

5. Coffee and Cultural Tours

Visit Kona coffee farms for tastings and tours.

Check out Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau National Historical Park to learn about Hawaiian culture.

6. Adventure Activities

Go ziplining over rainforests near Hilo.

Take a helicopter tour for stunning aerial views of the island.

7. Local Food and Farmers’ Markets

Try poke, malasadas, and shave ice.

Visit the Hilo Farmers Market or Kona Farmers Market.

8. Relax and Enjoy Aloha Spirit

Watch the sunset at Mauna Kea Beach or Kona Pier.

Treat yourselves to a luau or spa day.
